Today's paper of the day is Short Sellers, Short Squeezes, and Securities Fraud by Christine Hurt and Paul J. Stancil. This paper explores the questions of when and whether short sellers and other atypical traders should benefit from the securities fraud class action structure. It looks at the modern securities fraud class action lawsuit and how the Supreme Court assumed that almost all investors purchase and sell securities based on their belief that the market price is accurate. The article creates a theoretical and normative framework for atypical investors in the securities fraud paradigm and examines the implications of short sellers becoming more and more visible in class actions.
