Today's paper of the day is Infringement, Unbound by Sarah R. Wasserman Rajec. This paper argues that patent infringement doctrines which allow for expanded liability beyond the normal bounds of patent enforcement should be seen as a complement to the boundaries governing patent scope instead of a risk to the delicate balance of patent law. This article examines the requirements set forth by the patent in terms of subject matter, term, and scope and how recent enforcement rules have loosened them, such as by holding manufacturers and retailers liable for the infringement of their customers as “indirect” infringers. It argues for reform of patent infringement doctrines and that any changes must be moored to the same fundamental purposes that motivate the delicate balance patent law seeks.
