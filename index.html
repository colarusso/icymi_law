Today's paper of the day is Prior Bad Acts and Merger Review by Michael A. Carrier. This paper examines how antitrust agencies and courts should consider prior bad acts when assessing a merger of two firms in the pharmaceutical industry. It discusses how the 2010 Horizontal Merger Guidelines address collusion, and suggests a framework to consider prior bad acts when the markets are similar, there is a connection between the conduct and the markets covered by the merger, and there is sufficient proof of the prior bad acts. It also offers case studies and explains why, in mergers with prior bad acts, the array of potential relief should include not just a lawsuit to block the merger but also behavioral remedies.
