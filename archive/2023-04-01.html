Today's paper of the day is The Constitutional Limits of Criminal Supervision by Eric S. Fish. This article proposes a new solution to the constitutional puzzle of criminal supervision, where a judge can send someone to prison based on facts not proven to a jury. The solution is a conditional sentencing theory, which puts two important constitutional limits on criminal supervision. These limits are that a judge cannot retroactively change a supervision sentence and that a sentence for a supervision violation cannot exceed the statutory maximum for the underlying crime.
