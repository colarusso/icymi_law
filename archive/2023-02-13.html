Today's paper of the day is Myths and Reality of Patent Law at the Supreme Court by Paul R. Gugliuzza and Mark A. Lemley. The paper examines the conventional wisdoms about the Supreme Court's involvement in patent law and the trust the court places in the Federal Circuit and Solicitor General. The authors use quantitative and qualitative analysis of all patent cases since 1982 to assess the Supreme Court's impact on the patent system, and conclude that the court has been surprisingly deferential to the Federal Circuit.
