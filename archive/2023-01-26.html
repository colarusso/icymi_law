Today's paper of the day is ChatGPT Goes to Law School by Jonathan H. Choi, Kristin E. Hickman, Amy Monahan, and Daniel Schwarcz. In this study, the authors used the widely publicized AI model ChatGPT to generate answers on four real exams at the University of Minnesota Law School. They found that over 95 multiple choice questions and 12 essay questions, ChatGPT performed on average at the level of a C+ student, achieving a low but passing grade in all four courses. The authors discussed the implications of their results for legal education and lawyering, as well as providing example prompts and advice on how ChatGPT can assist with legal writing.
