Recently, an invited speaker was required to purchase insurance due to her last name being Trump, which FIRE believes is an example of viewpoint discrimination. The Supreme Court has held that speech cannot be financially burdened due to potential hostility, and FIRE has called on Trinity to revise their policy and adopt only content- and viewpoint-neutral criteria for campus events.

Today's paper of the day is Costly Designs by Sarah Burstein. The paper evaluates if the cost of design patent examination is a fatal flaw in the system, and if the costliness of design protection is really as problematic as the current literature suggests. It suggests that the cost of design patent examination may serve a valuable function by screening out bad design patents, and that the costliness of design patents may actually have a net positive effect on social welfare.
