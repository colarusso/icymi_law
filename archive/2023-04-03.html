Today's paper of the day is Culpability in Atrocity and the Role of Complicit Observer by Haley S. Anderson. This paper explores Randle DeFalco's book "Invisible Atrocities: The Aesthetic Biases of International Criminal Law," which argues that atrocity crimes are associated with an aesthetic of horrific spectacle. Anderson proposes an additional dimension of the atrocity aesthetic: culpability as imagined by the observer. Anderson suggests that our imaginations provide indicia of culpability by filling in the blank spaces in an image, and by doing so, we make ourselves complicit in the atrocity. Anderson argues that it is the sense of our complicity being intolerable that distinguishes the atrocity aesthetic from mere tragedies.
